IMPORTANT DISCLAIMER: The predictions of anatoxin concentrations and the risk of toxicity to humans and animals presented on this web page should not be used to make decisions about personal safety or the use of Russian River recreation opportunities. These predictions are based on a simple mathematical relationship between river water temperature, river flow, and air temperature that will never be absolutely accurate. My intention for the publication of this model is to help the Sonoma County Water Agency and the Sonoma County Board of Supervisors to exercise adaptive control of Russian River flow and thus avoid the bloom of blue-green algae and potential river toxicity.

Daily Derivative Graph of Russian River Low Flow Toxicity

This graph shows the predicted change in anatoxin concentration on a daily basis. For most of the year, the model equation predicts a negative change in anatoxin concentration. However, as the water temperature rises and the river flow decreases, the predicted change (or model derivative) becomes less negative. On August 1st 2015 the model predicted its highest change value. This was a positive 1.39 ug/L change in anatoxin due to three days of river flow less than 60 cfs with water temperature ~75 deg F.
