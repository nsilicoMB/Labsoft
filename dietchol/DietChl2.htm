Dietary Approaches to Lowering Cholesterol

Alex Sevanian and Michael B. Bolger

Number of visitors since 3/4/96:

Objectives:

At the end of the next X hr of continuing education, the pharmacist should be able to:

  1. Describe the latest evidence for the multifactorial nature of atherosclerosis, and the impact of dietary fats and fat soluble antioxidant vitamins.
  2. Review the mechanism of oxyradical formation, the impact on lipoprotein and cell oxidation, and how antioxidants inhibit these oxidative processes.
  3. Understand the evidence for the presence of oxidized lipoproteins in humans and their relationship to disease progression.
  4. Identify the latest antioxidant administration levels and how they impact on other lipid lowering therapies for the treatment of atherogenesis.

Outline:

  1. Impact of dietary fats on plasma cholesterol and atherosclerosis risk.
    1. Effect of dietary cholesterol content on plasma cholesterol levels
    2. The influence of polyunsaturated vs saturated fats on plasma cholesterol
    3. Cardiovascular disease-related deaths and fat intake
    4. Intake of foods and/or supplements with antioxidant vitamins and its impact on CHD
    5. The fat soluble vitamins - the special relationship to fat intake
  2. Evidence for oxidized lipoproteins in humans and animal models of atherosclerosis
    1. Modified lipoproteins in the vessel wall
    2. Modified lipoproteins in the plasma
    3. Relationships between the levels of modified lipoproteins and disease progression
    4. Relationships between the levels of antioxidant vitamins and disease progression
  3. Effects of oxidized lipids and oxidized low density lipoproteins on vascular cells (The Modified Lipoprotein Hypothesis)
    1. Cell injury and death
    2. Induction of inflammatory factors and cytokines
    3. Mechanisms for stimulated cell division
  4. The action of antioxidants and antioxidant vitamins
    1. Reaction of antioxidant compounds with oxyradical species
    2. Inhibition of lipid peroxidation by specific antioxidants: the special role of vitamin E and selenium
    3. How antioxidants inhibit low density lipoprotein oxidation
    4. Examples of inhibition of oxidant-induced responses by antioxidants
