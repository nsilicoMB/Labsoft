Rhodopsin
Bovine Rhodopsin

Receptors for adrenergic amines, serotonin, acetylcholine (muscarinic but not nicotinic), many peptide hormones, odorants, and even visual receptors (in retinal rod and cone cells) all belong to the serpentine family. The amino and carboxyl terminals of each of these receptors are located on the extracellular and cytoplasmic sides of the membrane, respectively. Different serpentine receptors resemble one another rather closely in amino acid sequences and in the locations of their hydrophobic transmembrane regions and hydrophilic extra- and intracellular loops, suggesting that all were derived from a common evolutionary precursor. Muscarinic receptors at these sites are G-protein coupled receptors.
